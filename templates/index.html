Introduction

Movements in the stock market are considered to be a leading indicator of the economy. This is because stock prices reflect expectations on future earnings, which are directly linked to future economic activity. The Alabama Stock Market Index tracks publicly traded companies headquartered within the State of Alabama and is designed to be used in conjunction with broader regional economic data to form a clearer picture of Alabama's overall economic trajectory.

In 2021, Alabama's GDP was approximately $248 billion, ranking it among the mid-tier states nationally. The state's economy is anchored by automotive manufacturing, aerospace and defense, financial services, materials, and healthcare — sectors well-represented in this index. The Alabama Stock Market Index was built using the same price-weighted methodology applied in the FGCU/RERI Southwest Florida Stock Index, allowing for methodological consistency and cross-regional comparisons.

The index includes 7 companies actively traded on U.S. exchanges, each domiciled in Alabama, with a minimum market capitalization exceeding $10 million. Index prices are sourced from Yahoo Finance historical data and the base date is set to January 2, 2020 = 100.

Index Beta

The Beta coefficient measures the relationship between the returns of the Alabama Index and the returns of the broader market (S&P 500). It is used to assess the volatility of the index and its correlation to overall market performance. Beta is calculated as: β = Cov(AL Index, Market) / Var(Market).

Methodology

The Alabama Stock Market Index is a price-weighted index (PWI), consistent with the Bloomberg Florida Index and the FGCU/RERI Southwest Florida Stock Market Index methodology. Price-weighting is used to minimize the disproportionate influence of penny stocks and to maintain methodological compatibility with established regional equity indices.

Price-Weighted Index Formula:
PWI = Σ(Stock Prices in Index) ÷ Number of Stocks

The index is normalized so that its value on the base date (January 2, 2020) equals 100. All subsequent values are expressed relative to that baseline, enabling direct percentage comparisons with the S&P 500.

Stock Screening Criteria

Companies were screened using SEC EDGAR corporate filings and Bloomberg equity screener tools, applying the following filters: (1) state of incorporation or principal executive offices located in Alabama; (2) actively traded on NYSE, NASDAQ, or NYSE American; (3) minimum market capitalization of $10 million; (4) continuous public trading history from January 2020.

Data Sources

Historical adjusted close prices are sourced from Yahoo Finance via the yahoo-finance2 Node.js package. S&P 500 data (ticker: ^GSPC) serves as the national benchmark. The browser reads a prebuilt JSON snapshot from data/al_index_data.json, which can be regenerated with npm run update-data. For Bloomberg Terminal users, prices can be sourced via the Bloomberg BLPAPI HistoricalDataRequest using the PX_LAST field on the relevant equity tickers.

Alabama Economic Context

Southwest Alabama's metro areas (Birmingham, Huntsville, Montgomery) represent the primary economic centers from which these companies originate. Huntsville has emerged as a major defense and aerospace corridor (ADTRAN, Redstone Arsenal contractors), while Birmingham anchors the state's financial services and healthcare sectors. Warrior Met Coal reflects the state's legacy energy/mining industry.

Conclusion

The Alabama Stock Market Index provides a focused lens on the equity performance of Alabama-headquartered companies relative to the national S&P 500 benchmark. From 2020 to 2022, the index demonstrated resilience across COVID recovery (2020–2021) and the Federal Reserve's rate-tightening cycle (2022), with a beta near 1.0 indicating near-market correlation with slightly differentiated sector exposure.

The index is dominated by Financial Services companies, which benefited from rising interest rate spreads in 2022–2023. Warrior Met Coal (HCC) provided significant commodity-linked upside during the 2021 energy commodity rally. ADTRAN's Technology weighting adds diversification from Alabama's growing Huntsville tech corridor.

As a leading economic indicator, movements in this index, read alongside labor force, taxable sales, and housing data on the RERI dashboard, help form a more complete picture of Alabama's regional economic trajectory. The index will be updated as new companies meeting the screening criteria become publicly traded.
